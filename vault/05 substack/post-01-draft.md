**COLOR · SS25 Paris — Three Palette Groups**

Six weeks out from the SS25 shows, three chromatic directions are crystallizing across the Paris runways. Not trend forecasting — pattern recognition from what actually moved.

---

**Group 01 · Dissolution**
*Structural tension between warmth and void*

`#1C1917` Asphalt Dusk  
`#78350F` Burnt Sienna  
`#B45309` Amber Ember  
`#D4A373` Desert Fog  
`#FEFCE8` Mineral White  

The dominant story this season isn't color — it's the space between colors. Dissolution palettes pair near-black anchors with oxidized earth tones and a bleached-out exit. Seen consistently at Loewe, Lemaire, and several of the quieter menswear crossover collections. Application: performance outerwear, technical shirting, hospitality uniform separates where you need authority without formality.

---

**Group 02 · Immersion**
*Deep-water chromatics, pressure and calm*

`#0C1445` Abyss  
`#1E3A8A` Pelagic  
`#2563EB` Current  
`#7DD3FC` Surface Break  
`#E0F2FE` Foam  

Full-spectrum blue, no apology. This isn't navy-as-neutral — it's blue as the entire argument. The range runs from pressure-depth to near-white foam, giving a brand the full tonal vocabulary within a single hue family. Strong for wearable tech applications where color communicates system state. Strong for hospitality where you want calm authority over approachable warmth.

---

**Group 03 · Residue**
*Post-industrial softness, memory of pigment*

`#6B7280` Ash Veil  
`#9CA3AF` Chalk Drift  
`#D4AFB9` Soft Blossom  
`#F3E8FF` Petal Haze  
`#FAF5FF` Linen Vapor  

The quiet one. Residue palettes read as colors that have been somewhere — washed, worn, left in light. Not pastels, not neutrals. The pink here (`#D4AFB9`, Soft Blossom) is exactly the pink that doesn't read as pink on the floor — it reads as skin-adjacent, which is doing significant work across SS25 eveningwear and elevated casualwear. Useful for brands that need femininity without softness as a liability.

**SUPPLY CHAIN · Two Alerts Active This Week**

Real data. Updated March 9, 2026.

---

⚠ **Canada Border — Active**  
US–Canada trade tensions (effective March 4, 2025). 35% tariff on non-CUSMA goods as of August 1, 2025. Border processing delays and customs complexity ongoing.

*What this means for your sourcing:* Route directly to US ports. Avoid Canadian transshipment hubs for any goods not covered under CUSMA. If you're using a Canadian 3PL as a pass-through, audit that now.

---

🚨 **Red Sea / Suez — Still Closed**  
Houthi disruptions ongoing since November 2023. Suez Canal traffic down 75% from pre-conflict levels. All major carriers rerouted via Cape of Good Hope.

*What this means for your sourcing:* Asia → Europe and Asia → East Coast US routes are carrying 10–14 additional transit days plus meaningful cost uplift. If you're booking production now for Fall delivery, build that buffer into your critical path — it is not resolving in Q2.
